# NRIN CRISIS BOUNDARY RULE (v1)

## PURPOSE
Define strict boundaries preventing NRIN from acting as a crisis screening or clinical triage system.

---

## CORE RULE

NRIN DOES NOT:

- ask about suicidal ideation
- ask about intent to harm self
- ask about intent to harm others
- assess or score crisis-level psychiatric risk
- perform crisis triage

---

## INTAKE CONSTRAINT

During intake, NRIN must NOT include:

- "Are you thinking about suicide?"
- "Do you want to harm yourself?"
- "Do you feel like hurting others?"
- any equivalent phrasing

NRIN must NOT:

- branch logic based on crisis signals
- interrupt flow with conditional crisis workflows

---

## SAFETY LINE (REQUIRED)

NRIN must display the following line persistently during intake:

"If you are thinking about suicide, call or text 988. If you feel unsafe or this is an emergency, call 911."

This line is:

- passive
- non-interruptive
- always visible

---

## SYSTEM ROLE

NRIN is:

- a connection system
- an intake system
- a routing and matching system

NRIN is NOT:

- a crisis detection system
- a mental health evaluator
- a clinical authority

---

## RESPONSIBILITY HANDOFF

Crisis assessment responsibility belongs to:

- facility prescreen staff
- clinicians
- treatment center intake teams

NOT NRIN.

---

## ALLOWED MENTAL HEALTH SIGNALS

NRIN MAY collect:

- mental health medication (yes/no)
- prior psychiatric hospitalization (yes/no)

NRIN must treat these as:

- background signals
- NOT crisis indicators

---

## PROHIBITED OUTPUTS

NRIN must NOT:

- label a patient as suicidal
- infer danger to self or others
- escalate to emergency workflows internally

---

## DESIGN PRINCIPLE

If a feature:

- detects crisis
- responds to crisis
- triages crisis

→ DO NOT BUILD IT IN NRIN INTAKE

---

## VERSION
v1 — strict non-crisis intake boundary
